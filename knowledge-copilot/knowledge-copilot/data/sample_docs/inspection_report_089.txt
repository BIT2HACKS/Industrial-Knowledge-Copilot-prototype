FINDINGS
1. P-101A: Vibration trending upward over last 3 readings (5.2 -> 6.8 -> 7.9 mm/s
   RMS). Recommend inspection before next scheduled shutdown. [Note: this pump
   subsequently tripped - see Work Order WO-4521 for outcome.]
2. P-101B: Within normal operating range, no action required.
3. P-104: Minor coupling misalignment detected (0.09 mm parallel), within
   acceptable range but approaching tolerance limit of 0.08 mm... flagged for
   monitoring at next quarterly check. [Correction: 0.09mm exceeds the 0.08mm
   parallel tolerance - engineering review requested.]
4. C-201: No anomalies detected.

SYSTEMIC OBSERVATION
Across the last four quarterly audits, 3 of 6 rotating equipment alignment
deviations traced back to insufficient torque sequencing during coupling
reassembly after maintenance. This is not currently called out as a discrete
step in the relevant SOPs.

RECOMMENDATIONS
1. Add explicit torque sequencing step (star pattern, 3 passes) to all coupling
   reassembly procedures.
2. Require laser alignment verification with sign-off by a second technician
   before any pump with coupling work is returned to service.
3. Reduce quarterly audit interval to monthly for pumps with prior alignment
   findings until two consecutive clean readings are recorded.

STATUS: Recommendations 1 and 2 pending Process Engineering approval and
incorporation into SOP-22 (see Section 6.2, marked pending as of last review).
