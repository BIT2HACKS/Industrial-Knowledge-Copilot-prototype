STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
SOP-22: Feed Pump Startup, Shutdown and Isolation (P-101A/B)
Revision: 4  |  Effective Date: 2024-01-10  |  Owner: Process Engineering

1. PURPOSE
This procedure covers safe startup, shutdown, and isolation of feed pumps P-101A
and P-101B in Unit 3, Process Area B.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all operations and maintenance personnel authorised to operate rotating
equipment in Process Area B.

3. PRE-STARTUP CHECKS
3.1 Confirm suction valve V-101 is fully open and discharge valve V-102 is closed.
3.2 Verify lubrication oil level in bearing housing is between MIN and MAX marks.
3.3 Confirm vibration monitoring system is active and trip setpoint is set to
    8.5 mm/s RMS (outboard bearing) per equipment datasheet EDS-P101.
3.4 Check coupling guard is installed and secured.

4. STARTUP SEQUENCE
4.1 Start pump motor from control room (DCS tag P101A-MTR).
4.2 Slowly open discharge valve V-102 over 30 seconds while monitoring discharge
    pressure. Target discharge pressure: 4.2 - 4.6 barg.
4.3 Monitor vibration and bearing temperature for 10 minutes post-start.

5. ISOLATION / CHANGEOVER TO STANDBY UNIT
5.1 On trip or alarm condition, start standby pump immediately using step 4.
5.2 Close discharge valve on tripped pump, then suction valve.
5.3 Tag out and log the isolation in the shift handover register.
5.4 Raise a work order for inspection before the isolated pump is returned to
    the available pool.

6. RETURN TO SERVICE AFTER MAINTENANCE
6.1 Any pump that has undergone coupling or bearing work must have laser shaft
    alignment verified against OEM tolerance (0.05 mm angular, 0.08 mm parallel)
    before startup.
