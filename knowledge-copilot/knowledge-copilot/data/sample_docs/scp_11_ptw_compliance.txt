SAFETY & COMPLIANCE PROCEDURE: SCP-11
Rotating Equipment Isolation and Permit-to-Work Requirements
Applicable Standard: OISD-STD-105 (Isolation of Process Equipment)
Revision: 2 | Effective Date: 2023-06-01

1. REGULATORY BASIS
This procedure implements the isolation requirements of OISD-STD-105 Section 4.3
and the Factory Act provisions on machinery guarding (Section 21-24) for all
rotating equipment in Process Area B.

2. PERMIT-TO-WORK REQUIREMENTS
2.1 Any maintenance work on rotating equipment requiring coupling removal,
    bearing access, or guard removal requires a Permit-to-Work (PTW) Type B
    (Mechanical Isolation) prior to commencing work.
2.2 PTW must be signed by the Area Shift Supervisor and the Maintenance
    Engineer performing the work.
2.3 Equipment must be electrically isolated and mechanically locked out per
    LOTO-04 before the PTW is issued.

3. COMPLIANCE EVIDENCE FOR AUDIT
3.1 Completed PTW forms must be retained for a minimum of 3 years and linked
    to the corresponding Work Order in the maintenance record system.
3.2 Any deviation from OEM alignment tolerance identified during maintenance
    (see equipment-specific SOPs, e.g. SOP-22 Section 6.1) must be logged as
    a non-conformance and tracked to closure.
3.3 Quarterly internal audit (see Inspection Report series, e.g. IR-2025-089)
    must confirm PTW compliance rate of 100% for Type B permits.

4. ESCALATION
Any rotating equipment with two or more alignment-related non-conformances
within a 12-month period must be escalated to the Unit Reliability Review
Board per OISD-STD-105 Section 6.

5. REVISION NOTES
Rev 2 clarified that alignment deviations are treated as reportable
non-conformances, following recurring findings on Unit 3 feed pumps.
